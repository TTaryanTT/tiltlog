"Tilt to control. Log to learn."

TiltLog is a browser-based gyroscope interface designed for real-time motion tracking and remote control applications. Built for mobile devices, TiltLog reads your smartphone's built-in orientation sensor and translates physical movement into precise, readable data.

What We Built and Why?

TiltLog was created as the control interface for a gyroscope-driven RC car project. The car is powered by a Raspberry Pi RP2040 microcontroller, and TiltLog serves as the wireless input device — your phone becomes the controller. Tilt left, the car turns left. Tilt forward, the car moves forward. No buttons, no joysticks — just natural movement.

Beyond RC control, TiltLog works as a standalone gyroscope testing and visualization tool. You can use it to measure your device's orientation sensitivity, visualize tilt on a live radar display, and read precise degree values in real time.

What TiltLog Does?

TiltLog accesses your device's built-in gyroscope sensor through the browser's Device Orientation API. It reads two axes of movement — left/right rotation (Y axis, gamma) and rotational heading (Z axis, alpha) — and displays them as live numerical values and a visual radar. All readings are shown as a delta from your chosen origin point, so you always see relative movement rather than raw absolute values.

Technology

TiltLog is built entirely with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript — no frameworks, no app store, no installation. It runs in any modern mobile browser and requires only a device with a gyroscope sensor.

The RC Car Project

The TiltLog RC car uses a Raspberry Pi RP2040 as its onboard controller. The car receives orientation data transmitted from the phone and converts tilt angles into motor speed and steering commands. The project explores the intersection of web technology, embedded systems, and wireless communication.
